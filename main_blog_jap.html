Think you know Japan? 10 common myths debunked...

  1. If you can't use chopsticks you'll starve.

    2. False. Ask for a fork.
    Yes. They have them.

  2. Japan is massively built up and overcrowded.

    3. True and false. Only the big cities are crowded. Most of Japan is still predominantly rural.

  3. Japan is a hi-tech nation.

    4. Sort of. I mean, they still use fax machines.

  4. There is no crime in Japan.

    5. On the surface? True. Most crime in Japan is organized and invisible. The streets are safe - day and night.

  5. Japanese people eat only fish.

    6. False. They love their fish, but there is a plethora of other foods that are delicious. Ramen, curry, okonomiyaki...

  6. Japan is prohibitively expensive.

    7. False. It isn't cheap compared to say, Thailand and Vietnam, but you'll be surprised. Trust me.

  7. You can't get around without knowing Japanese.

    8. False. The people are helpful, places are safe and with the Tokyo Olympics imminent, English signage is even more present.

  8. It's easy to offend the people.

    9. Mostly false, but some truth. Learn the most fuundamental things - take your shoes off before entering someone's home or a restaurant, for example.

  9. Credit cards are accepted everywhere.

    10. They are more prominent now than before, but get out of the bigger cities and chain stores and you'll find cash is still king.
    Carry some.

  10. The best time to visit is cherry blossom season.

    11. False. No, really.
    I won't lie, spring is magical. But be warned - tourists flock in their millions because they think the exact same thing.
    Just know that Japan is amazing in any season.
